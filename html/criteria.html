Understanding the Calculation

How is the 0.1 Increment Logic Applied?

The University uses a Linear Scaling System within grade bands. This ensures that every single mark you earn above the minimum threshold is rewarded.

The Statement: "To be increased by 0.1 for every 1 score above the minimum for the letter grade band."

Example: The "B" band starts at 70 marks with a 3.0 GP.

  • If you get 70, your GP is 3.0.
  • If you get 71, your GP is 3.1 (3.0 + 0.1).
  • If you get 73, your GP is 3.3 (3.0 + 0.3).
How to Calculate Semester GPA?

The Semester Grade Point Average (GPA) represents your academic performance for a single term. It is calculated by taking the "Weight" of each course into account.

To find your GPA, you first determine the Quality Points for each course by multiplying the Grade Point (based on your marks) by the Credit Hours of that course. You then sum these points and divide by the total number of credits taken during the semester.

Semester GPA Formula:

GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credit Hours) / Total Semester Credit Hours

Quick Calculation Example:

  • Course A (3 Credits): 4.0 GP → 12.0 Points
  • Course B (2 Credits): 3.0 GP → 6.0 Points
  • Total: 18.0 Points / 5 Credits = 3.60 GPA
How to Calculate CGPA?

The Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) is the official measurement of your overall academic standing across all semesters. A common misconception is that CGPA is calculated by averaging your semester GPAs (e.g., $(GPA1 + GPA2) / 2$). This is incorrect because different semesters may have different total credit loads.

Instead, the university uses a Weighted Average method. This means a 4-credit course has a greater impact on your final CGPA than a 2-credit course. To find the CGPA, you must divide the sum of all quality points earned throughout your degree by the total number of credits attempted.

CGPA Formula:

CGPA = (Σ Quality Points Earned) / (Σ Total Credits Attempted)

*Where Quality Points = (Grade Point × Course Credits)

Pro Tip: In this calculator, when you "Include Previous CGPA," the system automatically reverse-calculates your previous total points and adds them to your current semester's performance for an accurate cumulative result.
